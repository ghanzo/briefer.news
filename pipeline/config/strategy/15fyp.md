# 15th Five-Year Plan — 第十五个五年规划

## Origin

Currently being drafted; will be ratified at the 20th Central Committee's 5th Plenum (autumn 2026) and formally adopted by the National People's Congress in March 2027. Xi Jinping's January 20, 2026 speech at the provincial- and ministerial-level study seminar — republished verbatim in Qiushi on April 30, 2026 — laid out the political-economic framing. The 4th Plenum (October 2025) earlier identified 15FYP drafting as the leading task for the year.

## Core themes (as articulated through May 2026)

- **Manufacturing modernization as top strategic task.** Xi explicitly named the modernization of manufacturing — not services, not consumption — as the leading priority. Signals continuity of the "real economy" framing over financialization.
- **High-level tech self-reliance** (高水平科技自立自强). Semiconductors, basic research funding, AI infrastructure, scientific instruments. Driven by US export controls and the entity-list pressure.
- **New quality productive forces** (新质生产力, separate doc) as the integrating doctrine for AI + advanced manufacturing + scientific innovation.
- **Population shift response.** Xi's January 20 speech named demographic contraction, aging, and regional divergence as defining external constraints. Expect childcare / fertility / eldercare policy escalation.
- **Strategic opportunities and risks coexist.** The phrase 战略机遇和风险挑战并存 frames the period as more contested than the 14FYP era. US-China rivalry implicit throughout.
- **Higher-quality common prosperity** — continuing the 14FYP common-prosperity agenda but with explicit guardrails ("not egalitarianism, not welfarism").

## Current implementation focus

The 15FYP isn't operational yet, but every major policy release in 2026 is being framed as "preparing the conditions for the 15FYP" — the State Council's 2026 legislative plan, regulator rule-making (AI agents, algorithm negative list), and Beautiful China cadre-assessment all explicitly cite 15FYP groundwork.
