# Energy Security via Internal Transition — 能源安全新战略

## Origin

Xi Jinping's June 2014 Central Finance Committee speech introduced the **"Four Revolutions and One Cooperation"** (四个革命、一个合作) energy-security strategy: revolution in consumption, supply, technology, and institutions, plus international cooperation. The doctrine has steadily reframed Chinese energy security from import-source-diversification to **internal capacity build-out** as the primary instrument.

Dual circulation (separate doc, 2020) and 30/60 (separate doc, 2020) reinforced the inward turn: energy security increasingly means *less reliance on imports* rather than *more reliable imports*.

## Core themes

- **Internal capacity as security.** Reduce oil + gas import dependency by expanding domestic supply (renewable + nuclear + coal-as-floor) and electrifying demand (EVs, heat pumps, electric heavy industry).
- **Coal as strategic floor.** Coal-fired generation maintained as system backstop AND as domestic supply hedge against geopolitical oil-gas disruption. ~6.5 billion tons annual coal output target sustained through 2027.
- **Renewable industrial base = energy + jobs + export industrial policy.** Solar PV (Longi/Trina/JinkoSolar), wind (Goldwind, Mingyang), batteries (CATL, BYD), EVs (BYD, Geely) — all framed as energy-security industries, not just climate industries. Western export controls (US Section 301, EU CBAM) make this framing more urgent.
- **Nuclear acceleration.** Hualong One indigenous reactor standardized; build cadence ~8-10 reactors per year; targeting energy-mix diversification + technology export.
- **Grid investment** as the enabler — UHV DC lines moving renewable output from west/north to demand centers. State Grid + Southern Power Grid as strategic operators.
- **Strategic reserves expansion.** Crude oil + natural gas strategic reserves built up steadily; agricultural reserves part of the same security frame.
- **LNG sourcing diversification** continues (Qatar, Australia, Russia, US) but as marginal supply, not core dependency.

## Current implementation focus

- **NEA monthly capacity data** — additions of solar, wind, nuclear, coal-fired — primary visibility into the transition's pace.
- **State Council 2026 Legislative Plan** likely contains Energy Law revision and grid-investment legal framework.
- **EV / battery export surge** (April 2026: EVs +68% YoY) — industrial side of the strategy paying off.
- **Domestic shale + tight-gas push** — internal hydrocarbon supply expansion, even as renewable buildout accelerates.
- **Critical-minerals strategy** for battery supply chain (lithium, cobalt, nickel, rare earths) — DRC investments, Indonesia nickel processing, domestic recycling rules.
