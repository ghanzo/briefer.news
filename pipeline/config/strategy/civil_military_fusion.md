# Civil-Military Fusion — 军民融合发展战略

## Origin

Civil-military integration has been a Chinese theme since the 1990s, but Xi Jinping elevated it to a **national strategy** in March 2015 and institutionalized it in January 2017 with the **Central Commission for Integrated Military and Civilian Development** (中央军民融合发展委员会), chaired personally by Xi. The commission's establishment marks the formal start of the contemporary regime — the highest-level body specifically tasked with breaking down barriers between PLA acquisition, defense industrial base, and civilian high-tech.

## Core themes

- **Bidirectional dual-use tech transfer.** Civilian R&D into military applications (AI, drones, advanced materials, biotech, semiconductors), and military-grade R&D into civilian SOEs.
- **Defense-industrial base modernization.** SOE reform of the eight major defense conglomerates (AVIC, CASC, CASIC, NORINCO, CSIC/CSSC, CETC, CNNC, CNOOC offshore).
- **Talent and personnel mobility** — academics, engineers move between PLA-affiliated institutions (universities like Beihang, NUDT) and civilian firms.
- **Standards integration** — defense-grade testing applied to civilian dual-use product lines.
- **Private-sector defense participation.** "Civilian-to-military" (民参军) firms — private companies winning PLA contracts.

This is the rationale frame cited by US entity-list designations (BIS lists thousands of Chinese entities with CMF connections as the legal basis for export controls).

## Current implementation focus

- **AI for military / dual-use applications.** Embodied intelligence, autonomous systems, AI-for-Science National Pilot Bases (NDRC focus area, Shanghai AI Lab tour May 9, 2026).
- **Drone industry consolidation.** DJI plus military-adjacent UAV firms.
- **Defense SOE asset securitization** — listing portions of defense conglomerates on Chinese exchanges.
- **Sanction-induced acceleration.** US export controls strengthen the CMF rationale (we *must* fuse to substitute imports).
