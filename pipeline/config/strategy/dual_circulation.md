# Dual Circulation — 双循环

## Origin

Xi Jinping introduced 双循环 at a May 14, 2020 Politburo Standing Committee meeting and elaborated at a July 2020 entrepreneurs' symposium. The doctrine emerged amid the first wave of US tariffs + entity-list pressure + the COVID supply-chain shock. It was elevated to the 14FYP (separate doc) as a central organizing frame.

The formal phrasing: "以国内大循环为主体，国内国际双循环相互促进" — domestic circulation as the main body, with domestic and international circulation mutually reinforcing.

## Core themes

- **Domestic primary, international complementary.** Re-weights Chinese growth toward internal demand, internal supply chains, internal technology. Not autarky — selective international engagement remains.
- **Supply-chain resilience.** Indigenize critical inputs (semiconductors, energy, food, key technologies). Reduce vulnerability to external coercion.
- **Demand-side expansion at home.** Unleashing the "super-large market" (超大规模市场) — household consumption, services, urbanization.
- **High-quality international circulation.** Selective opening — pulling in foreign tech / capital that complements, deflecting that which threatens.
- **Anchor for industrial / financial policy.** Subsidies, state guidance, and policy bank lending tilt toward dual-circulation-aligned sectors.

## Current implementation focus

- **Service-economy expansion** (State Council Doc. No. 7, April 2026) targeting services output above 100 trillion yuan by 2030 — domestic-demand operationalization.
- **Africa zero-tariff regime** (May 1, 2026, for 53 African nations) — international-circulation expansion away from US-EU.
- **Export composition pivot** (EVs +68%, batteries +43%, wind +41%, robots +30% in April 2026) — high-value-add exports under industrial-circulation.
- **Foreign-exchange controls** under SAFE — managing capital flows under dual-circulation logic.
- **Domestic-content rules in procurement and standards.**
