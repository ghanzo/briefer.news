# New Quality Productive Forces — 新质生产力

## Origin

Xi Jinping introduced the phrase in September 2023 during a Heilongjiang inspection tour. Elevated to nationwide doctrine via a January 31, 2024 Politburo collective study session — the first time the Politburo studied an economic concept Xi had personally coined. By 2024-2025, every major industrial policy document references the term.

This is the doctrine that has effectively **replaced** "Made in China 2025" as the operative manufacturing-modernization frame — though MIC2025's sector list still informs implementation.

## Core themes

- **Productivity from a qualitatively different source.** Not labor + land + capital (Marx's classical inputs) — but innovation, scientific advance, advanced manufacturing.
- **AI + scientific innovation + advanced manufacturing** as integrated growth driver.
- **Replaces real-estate-driven growth.** The doctrine is partly an answer to "what comes after property?" The property sector's deflation in 2023-2024 forced the rethink.
- **High-level tech self-reliance** (高水平科技自立自强) — the operating word is "high-level," denoting frontier rather than catch-up.
- **Patient capital and basic research.** State Council documents emphasize long-horizon R&D investment over short-cycle commercialization.
- **Climate / new energy as opportunity** — EVs, batteries, solar, wind are positioned as new-quality productive force exemplars.

## Current implementation focus

May 2026 examples directly invoking the doctrine:
- CAC + NDRC + MIIT joint Implementation Opinions on AI Agents (May 8) — explicitly framed as "新质生产力" build-out.
- NDRC Chair Zheng Shanjie's Shanghai AI Lab tour (May 9) — quoted "AI as a new engine of growth."
- Qiushi commentary on April export composition (EVs +68%, batteries +43%, robots +30%) — framed as new-quality productive force outputs.
- State Council 2026 Legislative Plan including comprehensive AI law.
