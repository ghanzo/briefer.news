# Made in China 2025 — 中国制造2025

## Origin

Released May 2015 by the State Council under Premier Li Keqiang. Modeled on Germany's Industry 4.0 but explicitly more ambitious — targeting Chinese dominance, not parity, in ten strategic high-tech sectors by 2025. Became the political target of US trade actions starting 2018 (Section 301, entity list, semiconductor export controls).

After 2018-2020 the Chinese side stopped citing the document by name in public statements (under US pressure) — but the sector targets and instruments persisted. By 2023-2024, 新质生产力 (separate doc) effectively absorbed the policy frame.

## Core themes

Ten priority sectors:
1. Next-generation information technology (semiconductors, AI, quantum)
2. High-end CNC machine tools and robotics
3. Aerospace and aviation
4. Marine engineering equipment and high-tech ships
5. Advanced rail transportation
6. New energy vehicles (EVs)
7. Power equipment
8. Agricultural machinery
9. New materials
10. Biomedicine and high-performance medical devices

Operational mechanisms: state guidance funds, indigenous-content quotas, IP transfer requirements (later softened under WTO pressure), state-bank financing for strategic firms.

## Current relevance

MIC 2025 isn't formally dead — its sector targets have largely been hit or are on track in EVs (Chinese EVs are world-leading), batteries (CATL/BYD), high-speed rail, solar/wind. Semiconductors remain the conspicuous gap. The successor framework is **Made in China 2035** (alluded to but not formally codified) merged into 新质生产力 implementation.

## Today's coverage triggers

Bullets touching: EVs / batteries / solar / wind exports, semiconductor policy, robotics, aerospace (COMAC, J-series), high-speed rail expansion, biotech / pharmaceutical regulation, advanced materials, machine tools, sector-specific subsidies, "indigenous innovation" rhetoric, US entity-list responses.
