May 7, 2026

Cuba sanctions framework lands; Iran-China oil network targeted; DOJ opens UCLA admissions probe.

Voices

“[The Cuban government's policies] are repugnant to the moral and political values of free and democratic societies.”

Executive Order 14404 · May 11

“Racism in admissions is both illegal and anti-American, and this Department will not allow it to continue.”

AAG Harmeet K. Dhillon, DOJ Civil Rights · May 63

“America is producing as much natural gas as Russia, China and Iran combined.”

Energy Sec. Chris Wright, Three Seas Initiative Summit, Dubrovnik · April 286

Significant items

  • Cuba sanctions. Executive Order 14404 imposes a full sanctions regime on Cuban officials and on persons operating in the country's energy, defense, metals, financial, or security sectors; reaches adult family members of designated persons.1May 1 · Fed. Reg.
  • Iran-China oil. State and Treasury sanctioned Qingdao Haiye Oil Terminal — a China-based facility that handled tens of millions of barrels of sanctioned Iranian crude — plus three Iranian currency-exchange houses moving billions annually.2May 1 · State
  • DOJ v. UCLA. Civil Rights Division determined UCLA's David Geffen School of Medicine intentionally selected medical-school applicants by race; admitted Black and Hispanic applicants had, on average, lower academic qualifications than white and Asian applicants.3May 6 · DOJ
  • DPRK IT workers. Two U.S. nationals — Knoot of Nashville, Prince of New York — sentenced to 18 months for hosting laptop farms that let North Korean IT workers fraudulently work for U.S. companies; ~70 victim companies, $1.2M+ to DPRK.4May 6 · DOJ
  • CISA KEV. Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS out-of-bounds write (CVE-2026-0300) added to the catalog with active-exploitation evidence.5May 6 · CISA
  • Trump Peace Pipelines. Sec. Wright launched a U.S.-led MOU at the Three Seas Summit in Dubrovnik to expand natural-gas infrastructure across Central and Eastern Europe; concurrent deals: U.S.–Croatia civil-nuclear/SMR cooperation and Project Pantheon, a $50B+ American data center in Croatia.6Apr 28 · DOE
  • DRC–M23. DRC government and AFC/M23 representatives met April 13–17 in Montreux to advance the Doha Framework signed Nov 15, 2025; mediators: Qatar, U.S., Togo (AU mediator), AU Commission, Switzerland.7Apr 18 · State
  • Pax Silica. Norway became the 15th signatory of the U.S.-led Pax Silica Initiative for diversified critical-mineral supply chains.8May 6 · State
  • MFN drug pricing. 17 of the largest pharmaceutical manufacturers signed voluntary Most-Favored-Nation pricing agreements; the framework projects $529B in domestic savings over 10 years.9May · WH
