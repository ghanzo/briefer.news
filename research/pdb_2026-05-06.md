# The State of the Board — May 6, 2026

Mr. President, this week consolidates the Iran posture. **Project Freedom** is operating successfully: two U.S.-flag merchant ships have transited the Strait of Hormuz under our protective bubble, seven Iranian fast boats are now at the bottom of the Gulf, and Iran's broader naval, air, and missile-launch capabilities — destroyed during Operation Epic Fury — are not reconstituting on any near horizon. The economic flank holds: blockade plus sanctions are costing Iran an estimated $500 million per day, on top of 70% inflation and a currency in freefall. Sunday's UN Security Council resolution, drafted with Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Kuwait, and Qatar, places Russia and China in an awkward public position on Iranian piracy. Witkoff and Kushner remain engaged on the nuclear-material question; the buried 60%-enriched stockpile is still on the table.

The trade and procurement architecture moved Wednesday. You signed two consequential orders: federal procurement now defaults to fixed-price contracting, with ~$120 billion per year in cost-reimbursement consulting flagged for 90-day review; and TrumpIRA.gov stands up to standardize low-cost retirement-savings access for non-employer-covered workers. **USTR** opened four days of Section 301 hearings on the structural overcapacity of sixteen economies. March balance-of-payments data confirm the rerouting thesis is real — Taiwan ($20.6B) and Vietnam ($19.2B) deficits each now exceed our direct China deficit ($14.0B). **Justice** filed a fifth state-energy preemption complaint, this one against Minnesota, and the new Civil Rights Division Second Amendment Section sued Denver over its assault-weapons ordinance. **State** formalized a 20% hemispheric reweight of foreign assistance under a new Bureau for Disaster and Humanitarian Response, embedded with SOUTHCOM.

Smaller moves: Treasury sanctioned former DRC President Joseph Kabila on M23 support. The Peshawar consulate is phasing out — engagement transferred to Islamabad. A Latvian national was sentenced to 102 months for the Conti/Karakurt/Royal/Akira ransomware operation. Operation Iron Pursuit closed with 350+ arrests and 200+ child rescues across all 56 FBI field offices.

**Forward.** You meet Xi this coming week — Taiwan and human-rights framings expected. Lavrov initiated a call Sunday; the agenda was Russia-Ukraine and Iran. The UN Security Council vote on the Strait resolution is the next inflection.
