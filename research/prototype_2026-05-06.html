May 6, 2026 · No. 247

On Iran: U.S. drafts UN resolution; oil release reaches 172 million barrels.

A historic Strategic Petroleum Reserve drawdown reaches 172 million barrels; 23,000 civilian sailors remain stranded in the Gulf as a UN resolution is drafted in New York. President Trump is scheduled to meet President Xi Jinping the week of May 11.

Voices

“What is an act of war? Putting mines in the water.”

Sec. of State Marco Rubio, May 51

“These actions help move oil quickly into the market, address short-term supply pressures, and ensure that the Strategic Petroleum Reserve remains strong through the return of premium barrels.”

Kyle Haustveit, DOE Asst. Sec.3

“President Trump promised to unleash American energy dominance, and Minnesota officials cannot undermine his directive by mandating that their woke climate preferences become the uniform policy of our Nation.”

AAG Stanley Woodward, DOJ7

Significant items

  • Project Freedom. U.S. escorts U.S.-flag merchants through the Strait of Hormuz; two have transited and seven Iranian fast boats have been destroyed in defensive engagements.1
  • Operation Epic Fury. Concluded after destroying Iran's navy, air force, and missile-launch capability; Iranian inflation reported at 70%.1
  • Persian Gulf. Approximately 23,000 civilian sailors from 87 countries remain stranded; ten have died.1
  • UN Security Council. May 4, the U.S., Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Kuwait, and Qatar drafted a resolution requiring Iran to cease attacks, mining, and tolling.2
  • Trump–Xi summit. Scheduled for the week of May 11.1
  • SPR exchange. DOE issued an April 30 RFP for an emergency exchange of up to 92.5 million barrels — part of a coordinated 400-million-barrel IEA release, the largest in the Reserve's 50-year history.3
  • Section 301. USTR holding May 5–8 hearings on investigations into 16 economies' “structural excess capacity” in manufacturing.4
  • Trade deficit. March 2026 goods-and-services deficit was $60.3 billion; year-to-date down 55% from 2025. Largest country deficits: Taiwan $20.6B, Vietnam $19.2B, Mexico $16.4B, China $14.0B.5
  • Rubio–Lavrov call. Sec. Rubio spoke with Russian FM Sergey Lavrov on May 5 at Lavrov's request; reported topics: U.S.–Russia, Russia–Ukraine, and Iran.6
  • DOJ v. Minnesota. Federal-preemption complaint filed against Minnesota's state-court suits against energy companies — the fifth such filing.7
  • Bridger Pipeline. Presidential permit issued for a cross-border crude pipeline at the Montana–Canada international boundary.8
  • Hemisphere policy. State Dept. reweighted U.S. foreign assistance to allocate 20% to the Western Hemisphere through a new Bureau for Disaster and Humanitarian Response embedded with U.S. Southern Command.9
  • OFAC sanctions. Former DRC President Joseph Kabila sanctioned for material support to M23 and the Congo River Alliance.10
  • Ransomware sentencing. Latvian national sentenced to 102 months for participation in a Russia-based ransomware organization (Conti, Karakurt, Royal, Akira); estimated victim losses across 54+ companies in the hundreds of millions of dollars.11
  • Antitrust. DOJ announced investigations into U.S. meatpacking operations in a joint announcement with USDA and the White House Trade and Manufacturing office.12
  • CISA advisories. Five new industrial-control-system advisories published May 5: ABB B&R Automation Studio, Runtime, PVI; Hitachi Energy PCM600; Johnson Controls CEM AC2000.13
