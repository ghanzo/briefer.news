May 7, 2026

CENTCOM strikes Iranian targets after Hormuz transit attacks; Cuba sanctions framework lands.

Voices

“CENTCOM does not seek escalation but remains positioned and ready to protect American forces.”

U.S. Central Command · May 71

“The FY27 budget request is a definitive order to shift our Navy from a peacetime posture to a warfighting footing.”

Sec. of the Navy John Caudle · May 74

“[The Cuban government's policies] are repugnant to the moral and political values of free and democratic societies.”

Executive Order 14404 · May 13

Significant items

  • Hormuz attacks and strikes. Iranian forces launched missiles, drones, and small boats at three U.S. destroyers (USS Truxtun, USS Rafael Peralta, USS Mason) during a Hormuz transit. No U.S. assets struck. CENTCOM responded with strikes on Iranian missile/drone launch sites, command-and-control locations, and ISR nodes.1May 7 · CENTCOM
  • Gulf of Oman blockade. USS Abraham Lincoln launched an F/A-18 Super Hornet that disabled Iranian-flagged tanker M/T Hasna with 20mm cannon after multiple warnings; the U.S. blockade against ships entering or departing Iranian ports remains in full effect.2May 6 · CENTCOM
  • Cuba sanctions. Executive Order 14404 imposes a full sanctions regime on Cuban officials and on persons operating in the country's energy, defense, metals, financial, or security sectors; reaches adult family members of designated persons.3May 1 · Fed. Reg.
  • Navy FY27 budget. Department of the Navy released a $377.5 billion FY27 request — a 23% increase, part of Trump's $1.5T national-defense topline. Funds 34 new ships and 123 aircraft including doubled F-35 procurement (47 F-35s); $65.8B for shipbuilding (Columbia + 2 Virginia subs, Arleigh Burke destroyer, the new BB(X) "Battleship") and $22.6B for weapons (Tomahawks, Standard Missiles, PAC-3s).4May 7 · Navy
  • Iran-China oil. State and Treasury sanctioned Qingdao Haiye Oil Terminal — a China-based facility handling tens of millions of barrels of sanctioned Iranian crude — plus three Iranian currency-exchange houses processing billions annually.5May 1 · State
  • UK Skyhammer interceptors. UK Defence Minister Pollard oversaw successful Jordan trials of British-built Skyhammer interceptor missiles (range 30km, designed to counter Iranian Shahed-style drones); first tranche delivers to UK forces this month. UK Rapid Sentry and ORCUS systems still operating in Kuwait.6May 1 · UK MoD
  • DOJ v. UCLA. Civil Rights Division determined UCLA's David Geffen School of Medicine intentionally selected applicants by race; admitted Black and Hispanic applicants had lower academic qualifications, on average, than white and Asian applicants.7May 6 · DOJ
  • DPRK IT workers. Two U.S. nationals — Knoot of Nashville, Prince of New York — sentenced to 18 months for hosting laptop farms that let North Korean IT workers fraudulently work for U.S. companies; ~70 victim companies, $1.2M+ to DPRK.8May 6 · DOJ
  • CISA KEV. Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS out-of-bounds write (CVE-2026-0300) added to the catalog with active-exploitation evidence.9May 6 · CISA
