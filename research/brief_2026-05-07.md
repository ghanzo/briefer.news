# State of the Board — May 7, 2026

CENTCOM strikes Iranian targets after Hormuz transit attacks; Cuba sanctions framework lands.

---

## Significant items

- **Hormuz attacks and strikes.** Iranian forces launched missiles, drones, and small boats against three U.S. guided-missile destroyers — USS Truxtun, USS Rafael Peralta, and USS Mason — during a transit through the Strait of Hormuz to the Gulf of Oman. No U.S. assets were struck. CENTCOM responded by hitting Iranian missile and drone launch sites, command-and-control locations, and intelligence/surveillance/reconnaissance nodes.[^1]
- **Gulf of Oman blockade.** USS Abraham Lincoln launched an F/A-18 Super Hornet that disabled the rudder of Iranian-flagged tanker M/T Hasna using 20mm cannon fire, after the vessel ignored multiple warnings during a transit toward an Iranian port. The U.S. blockade against ships entering or departing Iranian ports remains in full effect.[^2]
- **Cuba sanctions framework.** Executive Order 14404 — signed May 1, published May 7 — imposes a full sanctions regime on Cuban officials and on any person operating in Cuba's energy, defense, metals, financial-services, or security sectors. The order reaches material supporters and adult family members of designated persons; foreign financial institutions transacting for designated persons can have U.S. correspondent accounts cut off.[^3]
- **Navy FY27 budget.** Department of the Navy released a $377.5 billion FY27 request — a 23% increase over FY26, part of Trump's $1.5 trillion national-defense topline submitted April 3. Funds 34 new ships and 123 new aircraft (including 47 F-35s, doubling F-35 procurement); $65.8B for shipbuilding (18 battle force + 16 auxiliary, including a Columbia-class submarine, two Virginia-class submarines, an Arleigh Burke destroyer, and the new BB(X) "Battleship" design); $22.6B for weapons including Tactical Tomahawks, Standard Missiles, and Patriot PAC-3s.[^4]
- **Iran-China oil sanctions.** State and Treasury sanctioned Qingdao Haiye Oil Terminal Co. — a China-based petroleum facility that has imported tens of millions of barrels of sanctioned Iranian crude — and three Iranian currency-exchange houses processing billions of dollars annually. Framed as part of "Economic Fury," State's named maximum-pressure campaign.[^5]
- **UK Skyhammer interceptors.** UK Minister for Defence Readiness Luke Pollard oversaw successful Jordan trials of British-built Skyhammer interceptor missiles (range 30km, speed 700km/h, designed to counter Shahed-style drones) produced by Cambridge Aerospace. First tranche being delivered to UK forces this month. UK's Rapid Sentry and ORCUS air-defense systems remain operating in Kuwait.[^6]
- **DOJ v. UCLA.** A year-long Civil Rights Division investigation determined that UCLA's David Geffen School of Medicine intentionally selected applicants based on race; admitted Black and Hispanic applicants had, on average, lower academic qualifications than their white and Asian counterparts.[^7]
- **DPRK IT workers.** Two U.S. nationals — Matthew Issac Knoot (Nashville) and Erick Ntekereze Prince (New York) — each sentenced to 18 months for hosting laptop farms that let North Korean IT workers fraudulently obtain remote employment with U.S. companies. Schemes generated $1.2M+ for the DPRK across nearly 70 victim companies.[^8]
- **CISA KEV.** CVE-2026-0300, a Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS out-of-bounds write vulnerability with active-exploitation evidence, added to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog. Federal civilian agencies must remediate per BOD 22-01.[^9]
