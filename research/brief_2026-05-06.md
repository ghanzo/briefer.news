# State of the Board — May 6, 2026

The U.S. coordinates a 400-million-barrel global oil release and presses a UN Security Council resolution against Iran's Strait of Hormuz blockade as a Trump-Xi summit approaches.

---

## Significant items

- The U.S. is conducting Project Freedom, escorting commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz; two U.S.-flag merchants have transited and seven Iranian fast boats have been destroyed in defensive engagements.[^1]
- The earlier Operation Epic Fury has concluded after destroying Iran's navy, air force, and missile-launch capability; Iranian inflation is reported at 70%.[^1]
- Approximately 23,000 civilian sailors from 87 countries remain stranded in the Persian Gulf; ten have died.[^1]
- On May 4, the U.S., Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Kuwait, and Qatar drafted a UN Security Council resolution requiring Iran to cease attacks, mining, and tolling.[^2]
- The Department of Energy issued an April 30 Request for Proposal for an emergency exchange of up to 92.5 million barrels of crude oil from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve, part of a coordinated 400-million-barrel release by IEA member nations — the largest SPR exchange in the Reserve's 50-year history.[^3]
- The U.S. Trade Representative is holding May 5–8 hearings on Section 301 investigations covering 16 economies' "structural excess capacity" in manufacturing.[^4]
- The March 2026 goods-and-services trade deficit was $60.3 billion (year-to-date down 55% from the same period in 2025); largest country deficits were Taiwan $20.6B, Vietnam $19.2B, Mexico $16.4B, China $14.0B.[^5]
- President Trump is scheduled to meet President Xi Jinping the week of May 11.[^1]
- Secretary of State Marco Rubio spoke with Russian Foreign Minister Sergey Lavrov on May 5 at Lavrov's request; reported topics were U.S.-Russia, Russia-Ukraine, and Iran.[^6]
- The Department of Justice filed a federal-preemption complaint against Minnesota's state-court suits against energy companies — the fifth such filing.[^7]
- A presidential permit was issued to Bridger Pipeline Expansion LLC for a cross-border crude-and-petroleum pipeline at the Montana-Canada international boundary.[^8]
- The State Department reweighted U.S. foreign assistance to allocate 20% to the Western Hemisphere through a new Bureau for Disaster and Humanitarian Response embedded with U.S. Southern Command.[^9]
- The Office of Foreign Assets Control sanctioned former DRC President Joseph Kabila for material support to M23 and the Congo River Alliance.[^10]
- A Latvian national was sentenced to 102 months in U.S. federal prison for participation in a Russia-based ransomware organization (Conti, Karakurt, Royal, Akira); estimated victim losses across 54+ companies were in the hundreds of millions of dollars.[^11]
- The Department of Justice announced antitrust investigations into U.S. meatpacking operations in a joint announcement with the Department of Agriculture and the White House Trade and Manufacturing office.[^12]
- CISA published five new industrial-control-system advisories on May 5 covering ABB B&R Automation Studio, ABB B&R Automation Runtime, ABB B&R PVI, Hitachi Energy PCM600, and Johnson Controls CEM AC2000.[^13]
