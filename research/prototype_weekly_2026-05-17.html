The week · U.S.

Week of 8 – 14 May, 2026

Washington went to Beijing with leverage and came back with a Taiwan warning.

Trump's state visit was the week's choreography; the harder story was that Xi set the agenda from the opening sentence. The coalition phase of the Iran war finally exceeded the striking phase, forty navies organizing what eight months of strikes never produced. By Friday it was clear: the week's most consequential sentence wasn't said in any briefing — it was Xi telling Trump that Taiwan is "fire and water."

Threads at week's end

The Iran war reached its seventy-sixth day with the coalition rather than the trigger becoming the story; forty navies in Hormuz outsize any single strike, and the question now is whether the Paris communiqué carries the next session in Riyadh. Trump landed in Beijing Wednesday and left Friday — the summit ended without a joint statement and with Taiwan as Xi's chosen sharp edge. Ukraine entered its fifth year unchanged in shape and worse in stamina; the week's drone strikes on Kyiv produced one Western aid tranche and no other movement.

The week's bullets

  • Xi names Taiwan first. In the open-press portion of Wednesday's bilateral, Xi told Trump "Taiwan independence and cross-Strait peace are as irreconcilable as fire and water" and that the U.S. must "exercise extra caution." Read in retrospect, this was the meeting's actual lead — not the trade-deal optics or the "constructive strategic stability" framing. The U.S. side, on the record, said nothing.1May 14 · MFA
  • Coalition Hormuz organization. Forty navies met in Paris to plan the Strait of Hormuz reopening; the UK contributed Typhoon jets, HMS Dragon, and additional Gulf air defenses. The Iran war moved into its coalition phase this week — a transition the U.S.-only press releases hadn't surfaced clearly.2May 12 · State
  • Pre-summit consolidation. Beijing published the AI-agents rulebook and the 14-bill 2026 lawmaking plan in the days before Trump landed. Editorially, the timing was the story: a regime arranging the room before the visiting president walked in.3May 8 · State Council
  • SPR fourth tranche. The Strategic Petroleum Reserve sent another tranche toward market with the Iran war's energy pressure mounting; the SPR is the U.S. domestic counterweight to the coalition's Strait-of-Hormuz work, and this week's release made the connection explicit.4May 13 · DOE
  • Iran oil determination renewed. The presidential determination keeping the squeeze on Iran-oil buyers was reissued Thursday — quiet, technical, and confirming the U.S. side intends the oil pressure to remain through the coalition's near horizon.5May 14 · FR
