## 1. Opening — Xi Jinping

### Framing: "transformation not seen in a century"

> "Transformation not seen in a century is accelerating across the globe, and the international situation is fluid and turbulent." *(MFA)*

### Three rhetorical questions — the Thucydides Trap framing

> "Can China and the United States overcome the Thucydides Trap and create a new paradigm of major-country relations?"
>
> "Can we meet global challenges together and provide greater stability for the world?"
>
> "Can we build a bright future together for our bilateral relations in the interest of the well-being of the two peoples and the future of humanity?" *(XH-2, PD)*

### Setting the year

> "I look forward to working together with you to set the course and steer the giant ship of China–U.S. relations, so as to make 2026 a historic, landmark year that opens up a new chapter in China–U.S. relations." *(PD)*

> "President Trump and I have had multiple meetings and phone calls and kept China–U.S. relations generally stable." *(XH-1)*

---

## 2. The "new vision" — constructive strategic stability

Xi announced the headline outcome of the meeting:

> "I have agreed with President Trump on a new vision of building a constructive China–U.S. relationship of strategic stability." *(MFA, PD)*

Xi defined the term in four clauses:

> "Positive stability with cooperation as the mainstay,
> a sound stability with moderate competition,
> a constant stability with manageable differences,
> and an enduring stability with promises of peace." *(XH-1, MFA)*

Supporting principles Xi stated:

> "Looking back at the course of China–U.S. relations, whether or not we could have mutual respect, peaceful coexistence, and win-win cooperation is the key." *(XH-1)*

> "We must make it work, and never mess it up." *(XH-1)*

> "Both China and the United States stand to gain from cooperation and lose from confrontation." *(XH-1)*

> "Where disagreements and frictions exist, equal-footed consultation is the only right choice." *(PD)*

> "The two sides should implement the important consensus we have reached, and make better use of communication channels in the political, diplomatic and military-to-military fields." *(PD)*

---

## 3. Trade and openness

> "China–U.S. economic and trade ties are mutually beneficial and win-win in nature." *(MFA)*

> "China will only open its door wider." *(MFA, XH-1)*

The economic and trade teams' talks produced "generally balanced and positive outcomes" — "good news for the people of the two countries and the world." *(XH-1, MFA)*

---

## 4. Taiwan

> "The Taiwan question is the most important issue in China–U.S. relations." *(MFA, PD)*

> "'Taiwan independence' and cross-Strait peace are irreconcilable as fire and water." *(MFA, PD)*

> "The U.S. side must exercise extra caution in handling the Taiwan question." *(MFA)*

> "If it is handled properly, the bilateral relationship will enjoy overall stability." *(XH-1)*

This was the sharpest passage of the open-press portion — widely characterized in Western coverage (CNN, NPR, ABC, CNBC) as a "warning."

---

## 5. Opening — Donald Trump

The Chinese-side readouts captured these short open-press lines from Trump. They are the entirety of what's quoted on the record from the U.S. side as of this compilation.

> "It was a great honor to pay a state visit to China." *(MFA)*

> "President Xi and I have had the longest and greatest relationship the presidents of the two countries have ever had." *(MFA)*

> "President Xi is a great leader, and China is a great country." *(MFA, PD)*

> "I have tremendous respect for President Xi and the Chinese people." *(PD)*

> "Together, we can do a lot of big and good things for the two countries and the world." *(MFA, PD)*

> "Today is a fantastic day." *(XH-1)*

> "The relationship between China and the USA is going to be better than ever before." *(WH-V, Reuters/AP coverage)*

Themes Trump emphasized per the Chinese-side readouts (paraphrased — not direct quotes):

- Working with Xi to strengthen communication and cooperation and properly handle differences
- Making bilateral relations "better than ever before"
- Deepening mutual understanding and strengthening friendship between the two peoples

---

## 6. Agreed outcomes

1. **The "new vision"** of constructive China–U.S. relations of strategic stability — strategic guidance for "the next three years and beyond." *(MFA, XH-1)*
2. **Mutual support for hosting** APEC Economic Leaders' Meeting and G20 Summit (both fall under 2026). *(MFA)*
3. **Continued economic and trade dialogue** — teams to implement the "balanced and positive" outcomes. *(XH-1)*
4. **Communication channels** in political, diplomatic, and military-to-military fields to be put to "better use." *(PD)*

**No joint statement** has been issued on day 1.
