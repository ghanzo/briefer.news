## Significant items (U.S. government sources)

- **Hormuz attacks and strikes.** Iranian forces launched missiles, drones, and small boats at three U.S. destroyers — USS Truxtun, USS Rafael Peralta, and USS Mason — during a Hormuz transit. No U.S. assets struck. U.S. forces responded with strikes on Iranian missile/drone launch sites, command-and-control locations, and ISR nodes.[^1]
- **Gulf of Oman blockade.** USS Abraham Lincoln launched an F/A-18 Super Hornet that disabled Iranian-flagged tanker M/T Hasna with 20mm cannon after multiple warnings; the U.S. blockade against ships entering or departing Iranian ports remains in full effect.[^2]
- **Cuba sanctions framework.** Executive Order 14404 — signed May 1, published May 7 — imposes a full sanctions regime on Cuban officials and on persons operating in Cuba's energy, defense, metals, financial-services, or security sectors; reaches material supporters and adult family members of designated persons.[^3]
- **Navy FY27 budget.** Department of the Navy released a $377.5 billion FY27 request — a 23% increase, part of Trump's $1.5T national-defense topline. Funds 34 new ships and 123 aircraft including doubled F-35 procurement; $65.8B for shipbuilding (Columbia + 2 Virginia subs, Arleigh Burke destroyer, the new BB(X) "Battleship") and $22.6B for weapons.[^4]
- **Iran-China oil sanctions.** State and Treasury sanctioned Qingdao Haiye Oil Terminal — a China-based facility handling tens of millions of barrels of sanctioned Iranian crude — plus three Iranian currency-exchange houses processing billions annually.[^5]
- **UK Skyhammer interceptors.** UK Defence Minister Pollard oversaw Jordan trials of British-built Skyhammer interceptor missiles (range 30km, designed to counter Iranian Shahed-style drones); first tranche delivered to UK forces this month.[^6]
- **DOJ v. UCLA.** Civil Rights Division determined UCLA's David Geffen School of Medicine intentionally selected applicants by race; admitted Black and Hispanic applicants had lower academic qualifications than white and Asian counterparts.[^7]
- **DPRK IT workers.** Two U.S. nationals — Knoot of Nashville, Prince of New York — sentenced to 18 months for hosting laptop farms enabling fraudulent North Korean IT workers; ~70 victim companies, $1.2M+ to DPRK.[^8]
- **CISA KEV.** Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS out-of-bounds write (CVE-2026-0300) added with active-exploitation evidence.[^9]

## Wider Lens — *non-gov sources*

> Items below are sourced from major news outlets, parliamentary
> research, and shipping-industry trade press. They report
> developments that U.S. government press releases have not yet
> acknowledged — including foreign-government positions, mediation
> backchannels, and commercial-shipping incidents.

- **Day 69 of the war.** Today's Hormuz exchange is escalation within a fragile ceasefire dating to April 8. The war began February 28, 2026, when the U.S. and Israel launched ~900 strikes in 12 hours; Iranian Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei was killed in an Israeli airstrike that day. The U.S. has named the air-war phase "Operation Epic Fury."[^a] [^b] [^c]
- **Pakistan-mediated talks intensifying.** Pakistan brokered the April 8 ceasefire; Pakistani Foreign Ministry spokesperson Tahir Andrabi told NPR mediation is in "direct communication" with both sides. Reuters reports the parties may be close to signing a one-page memorandum: Iran halts uranium enrichment for 12+ years, the U.S. lifts sanctions and releases frozen Iranian assets, and both sides reopen Hormuz within 30 days of signing.[^d] [^e]
- **French container ship attacked.** French shipping group CMA CGM confirmed its Maltese-flagged container ship *San Antonio* was hit by missiles transiting the Strait of Hormuz on May 5; eight Filipino crew members were wounded. The IMO logged it as the **32nd attack on commercial shipping** since the war began. Approximately 20% of global oil trade is disrupted; hundreds of vessels are stranded in the region.[^f] [^g]
- **UK declines U.S. blockade.** PM Keir Starmer told BBC radio that "we are not supporting the blockade" and that the UK "is not getting dragged in" to the war. The UK is leading a 40-nation coalition with France's Macron to reopen Hormuz; Starmer warned the UK economy will not "return to normal" once the strait reopens.[^h] [^i]
